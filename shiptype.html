E.B. Allen

The E.B. Allen first launched in 1864 by H.C. Piersons at Ogdensburg, New York. On November 20, 1871 the ship collided with the Newsboy 2 miles southeast of Thunder Bay Island during a voyage to deliver grain. It remains underwater to this day, where most of the decking is gone and the masts are broken.

J.J. Audubon

The J.J. Audubon was built in 1854 by G & W Jones of Cleveland Ohio. On October 20, 1854 it collided into the DEFIANCE. It currently lies in 178 feet of water north of the Presque Isle.

DEFIANCE

The DEFIANCE was built in 1848 by Captain Roby at Perryburg, Ohio. On October 20, 1854 around 1pm the ship was run into by the J.J. Audubon off of Preque Isle. It remains underwater, but much of its mast has been well preserved and there is little damage to the hull.

Studying shipwrecks

Studying shipwrecks is a wonderful way to learn about the history and structures of a ship. Additionally, the information gathered from these accidents could provide vital information for future ships in terms of safety and caution. These are only a few examples of the many possible shipwrecks to learn about all around the world. If you're interested in learning more, we highly encourage taking a look at the scuba diving clubs found on the home page. There you will meet people with similar interests and perhaps you may even encounter a shipwreck too!
